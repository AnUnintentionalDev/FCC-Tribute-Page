Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Something about Kalam Sir

Born
15th Oct 1931

Abdul Kalam was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu on 15th Oct 1931

College and Graduation

Went to attend Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras and graduated in Physics in 1954.

Madras Institute of Technology
1956

Went Madras to study Aerospace Engineering in Madras Institute of Technology

Enter ISRO
1969

Abdul Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vechile (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980.

Reasearch Centers Visit
1963-1964

During this period he visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland etc.

In 1970's Kalam also directed two projects, 'Project Deveil' and 'Project Valiant'.

Kalam played a major part in developing many missiles under the mission including 'Agni' an intermediate range ballistic missile and 'Prithvi' the tactical surface-to-surface missile.

Cheif Scientific Adviser
July 1992 to Dec 1999

Served as Chief Scientific Adviser to the prime minister and secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation

Kalam-Raju Stent
1998

Kalam along with cardiologist Soma Raju developed a low cost coronary stent, named the Kalam-Raju Stent

Kalam-Raju Tablet
2012

Again with Soma Raju, designed a rugged tablet computer for health care in rural areas, named the Kalam-Raju Tablet

11th President of India
25th July 2002

Kalam Sir became the 11th President of Republic India.
